REVIEW

Louie Misto April 16, 2026

The Maze Runner is the best dystopian movie I’ve ever seen. Right from the start its intense from the first scene. It slowly reveals what’s going on but is not to complex to be able to understand. The maze part and the goal to escape is a very interesting part of the movie and makes it very interesting. The grievers also are a good touch to the movie because they give them something to avoid and be scared of. Newt and Minho and other of the other characters give the group good diversity and added different personalities and helps the group feel more real. Teresa showing up was also a good addition to the movie and the group because it added more questions and a mystery to solve. In the end, if you like dystopian and these type of movies this one is definitely for you.

TRIVIA

  • Thomas Brodie-Sangster (Newt) was the only actor in the largely British cast allowed to keep his native accent, and he would place a small rock in his shoe to help him remember to limp
  • Actor Blake Cooper, who played Chuck, landed his role by relentlessly campaigning on Twitter, eventually sending director Wes Ball a tweet saying he was made for the role
  • Director Wes Ball originally rejected Dylan O'Brien for the role of Thomas because his hair was too styled ("Teen Wolf hair") until he saw a photo of him with a shaved head .
