REVIEW

Louie Misto April 16, 2026

Charm City Kings is a good movie showing what life is like for kids growing up in Baltimore. The story follows Mouse, who dreams of joining a dirt bike crew, and you can tell right away how important that is to him. I liked how the movie didn’t try to sugarcoat anything, it shows both the fun and the danger of that lifestyle. Mouse is surrounded by bad influences and he’s trying to figure out what he wants to do and who he wants to be. His brother lost his life from the bike scene and Mouse still wants to follow in his foot steps. Blax, the leader of the crew, is interesting because he kind of acts like a mentor but also leads Mouse into risky situations. The relationships in the movie feel genuine, especially between Mouse and his family, which adds more emotion to the story and shows what he’s risking. Overall, I think the movie does a great job mixing action with real-life struggles. Its not only about the bike life scene, its more about choices, growing up, and dealing with pressure from your environment.

TRIVIA

  • The movie is a narrative adaptation of the 2013 documentary 12 O'Clock Boys by Lotfy Nathan, which chronicles the real-life urban dirt bike culture in Baltimore
  • To ensure authenticity, the filmmakers hired real Baltimore dirt bike riders to perform the dangerous stunts and wheelies seen in the movie.
  • The film was co-written by Academy Award winner Barry Jenkins (Moonlight) and had Will and Jada Pinkett Smith serving as executive producers.
